Governor Susana Martinez to Announce NGL Energy Partners Locating in Milan, Creating 115 Jobs
Press Release
By: Susana Martinez
Date: Oct. 16, 2014
Location: Milan, NM

Today, Governor Susana Martinez will announce that transloading firm NLG Energy Partners will invest $52 million in a facility in Milan to serve the Four Corners energy industry. This facility will create 115 full-time jobs with a yearly average salary of $92,600 plus benefits. Gov. Martinez will be joined by Economic Development Secretary Jon Barela, State Senator George Mu√±oz, Village of Milan Mayor Pro Tem Vivian Brumbelow, Cibola County Commissioner Walter Jaramillo, Cibola Communities Economic Development Foundation and NGL Energy Partners CEO Mike Kimball for the announcement.
"We are thrilled that NGL is locating in Milan and will further grow the state's transportation and logistics industry," said Gov. Martinez. "New Mexico is geographically positioned to become a leading logistics hub and we'd like to see this region replicate the success that's taking place at our southern border with the location of Union Pacific there."
NGL Energy Partners is a Delaware-based business that serves the energy industry at various points throughout the supply chain. The Milan truck offloading/rail loading facility's operations will begin in the third quarter of 2015.
"New Mexico is working hard to diversify our private sector, and logistics and transportation is a top recruitment sector as we work to reduce New Mexico's reliance on the federal government," said Economic Development Secretary Jon Barela. "Jobs in this industry typically pay well, are helping to grow our rural communities, and is an increasing in sector in our country."
