Governor Susana Martinez Signs Additional Economic Development Legislation
Press Release
By: Susana Martinez
Date: April 5, 2011
Location: Santa Teresa, NM

Governor Susana Martinez announced today that she has signed
multiple pieces of economic development legislation into law. Governor Martinez signedHouse Bill 8, providing funding for economic development training; Senate Bill 84, extending the 55% jet fuel gross receipts and compensating tax deduction for five years, through the end of fiscal year 2017; House Bill 273, extending the eligibility period for the research and development small business tax credit from fiscal year 2012 to fiscal
year 2015; and Senate Bill 424, authorizing the creation of a new enterprise fund to support and maintain the New Mexico 9000 program.
"In order to promote economic development in New Mexico, we must promote an environment that allows for growth, investment, and job creation," said Governor Martinez. "I am committed to making New Mexico a friendlier place to do business and doing what it takes to get our economy back on track."
Earlier today, Governor Martinez was in Santa Teresa to sign key economic development items that included a locomotive fuel tax exemption, as well as two key bills to promote development along the southern New Mexico border.
