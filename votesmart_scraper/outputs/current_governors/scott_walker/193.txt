Governor Walker Honors Members of the United States Armed Forces in Memorial Day Statement
Press Release
By: Scott Walker
Date: May 27, 2016
Location: Madison, WI

Governor Scott Walker released the following statement today in anticipation for Memorial Day on Monday:
For the citizens of our state and nation, Memorial Day represents the beginning of summer, and while it is a day for spending times with loved ones, it's important we remember the true meaning of this holiday. Every day in the United States, brave men and women make the decision to serve and protect their country by joining the Armed Forces. On Memorial Day, we commemorate those who paid the ultimate price for our safety by laying down their lives. May God bless those who died while safeguarding our freedoms in the United States Armed Forces - their dedication, courage, and honor will never be forgotten.
Governor Walker will attend the Southern Wisconsin Veterans Memorial Cemetery Memorial Day Ceremony in Union Grove on Sunday as well as the Veterans Memorial Park Memorial Day Ceremony in DeForest on Monday.
Governor Walker also issued Executive Order #199 ordering that the flag of the United States and the flag of the State of Wisconsin be flown at half-staff on Memorial Day.
