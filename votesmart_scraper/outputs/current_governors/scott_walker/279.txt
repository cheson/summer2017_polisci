Governor Scott Walker Signs Assembly Bill 771 Into Law
Press Release
By: Scott Walker
Date: March 21, 2016
Location: Darlington, WI

Governor Scott Walker signed Assembly Bill 771 into law today at the Lafayette County Courthouse in Darlington.
"The safety and well-being of Wisconsin citizens is of the utmost importance," Governor Walker said. "Assembly Bill 771 provides much-needed funds to the Lafayette County Sheriff's Department for drug law enforcement. These funds enable the Sheriff's Department to better coordinate their law enforcement efforts with those of the surrounding counties, thus allowing their officers to better protect the citizens of Wisconsin."
Assembly Bill 771 -- directs the Department of Justice (DOJ) to make grants to Lafayette County for drug prevention and law enforcement services in the amount of $20,000 in fiscal year 2015-2016 and in the amount of $50,000 in fiscal year 2016-2017. Authored by Representatives Todd Novak (R -- Dodgeville) and Travis Tranel (R -- Cuba City), and Senator Howard Marklein (R -- Spring Green), the bill passed the Assembly with a vote of 84-14 and was concurred by the Senate with a vote of 29-3. It is Act 271.
