Governor Scott Walker Attends Monroe County Economic Development Conference
Press Release
By: Scott Walker
Date: Feb. 25, 2016
Location: Fort McCoy, WI

Governor Scott Walker delivered remarks today at the 5th Annual Monroe County Economic Development Conference, held at the Wisconsin Military Academy in Fort McCoy.
"More people working than at any other point in our state's history," Governor Walker said. "In 2015, we saw the largest monthly jobs gain since April of 1992, and here in Monroe County, the unemployment rate is 4.4 percent. Our state's economy has seen considerable growth, and our workforce is stronger than ever. As we continue to invest in Wisconsin's future, we are working to ensure every citizen of Wisconsin who wants a job has the skills and knowledge they need to find a job."
The Monroe County Economic Development Conference highlights the current economic climate of Monroe County. Major topics of discussion throughout the day include workforce issues, transportation and roads, supervisors and leadership, business retention and growth, and additionally spotlights successful businesses in Monroe County. The conference draws attendees from the 14 surrounding counties and features 31 exhibitors, including the Department of Workforce Development (DWD), the Department of Administration (DOA), the Department of Natural Resources (DNR), and Western Technical College.
