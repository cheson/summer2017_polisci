Building Commission Approves Projects
Press Release
By: Scott Walker
Date: Feb. 17, 2015
Location: Madison, WI

Today, the State of Wisconsin Building Commission approved several key projects across the state including, but not limited to:
The design report and authority to expand the Copper Lake School Segregation Unit in Lincoln County;
Upgrades to the Governor Tommy Thompson and Art A. Oehmcke Fish Hatcheries, which are part of Governor Walker's Walleye Initiative; and
Various maintenance and repair projects all across the state.
"Today we took a step forward on a number of important projects, including the Walleye Initiative I proposed in 2013," said Governor Scott Walker. "I would like to thank Building Commission members for taking action on all of these projects, which will efficiently improve facilities all across Wisconsin."
The Building Commission is chaired by Governor Walker and made up of the following members:
State Senator Terry Moulton;
State Senator Jerry Petrowski;
State Senator Janis Ringhand;
State Representative Rob Swearingen;
State Representative Mark Born;
State Representative Robb Kahl; and
Citizen member Bob Brandherm.
