Governor Scott Walker Attends EAA AirVenture: "The World's Greatest Aviation Celebration"
Press Release
By: Scott Walker
Date: July 20, 2015
Location: Madison, WI

Governor Scott Walker attended EAA AirVenture Oshkosh 2015 today at Wittman Regional Airport in Oshkosh. The annual event attracts hundreds of thousands of visitors and more than 10,000 aircraft from more than 60 countries across the globe.
"EAA AirVenture is something I look forward to attending every year," Governor Walker said. "It's a fun, exciting event for Wisconsin, and it's great for our tourism industry. EAA AirVenture has an annual economic impact of more than $110 million on the state and local economy, attracting people from all over the world to our great state to one of the best aviation events in the world."
Running annually since 1953, EAA AirVenture has been held in Oshkosh since 1969. This year's EAA AirVenture features: 10,000 airplanes, 2,500 show planes, more than 800 exhibitors, 1,000 forums and workshops, the F-35 U.S. air show debut, World War II and Apollo 13 tributes, a salute to our Veterans, and a Dierks Bentley concert. It runs Monday, July 20 through Sunday, July 26.
The Experimental Aircraft Association (EAA) was founded by Paul Poberezny, a veteran of World War II and the Korean War, in his basement in Milwaukee. It has since grown to include over 800 chapters across the United States and Canada.
In honor of EAA AirVenture Oshkosh 2015, Governor Walker has proclaimed July 20, 2015 -- July 26, 2015 as Aviation Week.
