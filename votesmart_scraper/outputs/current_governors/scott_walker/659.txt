Governor Scott Walker Attends Annual Midwest US--Japan Association Conference
Press Release
By: Scott Walker
Date: Sept. 8, 2014
Location: Des Moines, IA

Governor Scott Walker is attending the Midwest U.S.--Japan Association Conference to build trade ties between Wisconsin and Japan. This year, the annual conference is held in Des Moines, IA. It began Sunday, September 7th and ends Tuesday, September 9th.
"This annual conference plays an important role in moving Wisconsin's economy forward and continuing to build our relationship with Japan," Governor Walker said. "Japan is one of our top export destinations. I really enjoy this great opportunity to meet with Japanese companies and discuss the high-quality products that Wisconsin offers."
Governor Walker is accompanied by Reed Hall, CEO and secretary of the Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation. The Midwest U.S.--Japan Association Conference is held annually to discuss the growth and progress of economic relations of the American Midwest and Japan. Nine states including Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, Nebraska, Ohio, and Wisconsin are members of the Association.
Japan is Wisconsin's fourth largest export destination, with exports totaling $934 million in 2013. Japan is the top purchaser of Wisconsin medical and scientific instruments. There are over 25 Japanese affiliated firms in Wisconsin, including Kikkoman Corporation, which has had a presence in Wisconsin for the last 40 years. In addition to exports, several Wisconsin companies have operations in Japan, including Briggs and Stratton, Rockwell Automation, Harley-Davidson Motor Company, and Manpower.
