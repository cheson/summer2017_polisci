Governor Doug Ducey Appoints Bryan Chambers to the Gila County Superior Court
Press Release
By: Doug Ducey
Date: July 16, 2015
Location: Phoenix, AZ

Governor Doug Ducey today announced the appointment of Globe attorney Bryan Chambers to serve the remainder of Judge Peter Cahill's term, which will end in 2016, as a Gila County Superior Court Judge.
Mr. Chambers is the civil bureau chief and a deputy county attorney for the Gila County Attorney's Office. He has a wide range of experience in civil, criminal, and family law and is presently serving as president of the State Bar of Arizona.
"Bryan is well-known for his legal abilities and for having a diverse legal background," said Governor Ducey. "I am impressed with Bryan's involvement with the State Bar of Arizona, ultimately rising to the position of president. I'm confident he will make an excellent contribution to the people of Gila County."
Mr. Chambers has been a resident of Gila County for over 23 years.
He obtained his undergraduate degree from Brigham Young University in 1989 and his law degree from Brigham Young University J. Reuben Clark Law School in 1992.
