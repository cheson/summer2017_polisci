Governor Doug Ducey Makes Economy-Focused Trip To Mexico City
Press Release
By: Doug Ducey
Date: June 17, 2015
Location: Phoenix, AZ

Governor Doug Ducey today arrived in Mexico City for a packed week of meetings with Mexican officials and business leaders in an effort to bolster our economic and diplomatic relationships with the country.
"I'm happy to be here representing Arizona and all our state has to offer," said Governor Ducey. "Mexico is Arizona's largest trading partner and we both can benefit greatly from an enhanced relationship. Since taking office, I've spoken and met with many Mexican elected officials and business leaders; we're all eager to work together to ensure both our economies prosper."
This is Governor Ducey's first trip to Mexico since taking office in January 2015.
In 2014, Arizona's total exports to Mexico were valued at $8.6 billion, and imports from Mexico were valued at $7.3 billion. Visitors from Mexico spend an average of $7.3 million per day in the State of Arizona.
