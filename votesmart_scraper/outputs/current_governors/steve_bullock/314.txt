Montana's Unemployment Rate at 4.5%
Press Release
By: Steve Bullock
Date: Nov. 21, 2014
Location: Helena, MT

Montana's unemployment rate again dropped slightly in October, down 0.1 percentage points to 4.5%. The unemployment rate has decreased by 0.8 percentage points since January. The U.S. unemployment rate also decreased by 0.1 percentage points to 5.8%.
"As we head into the holiday season, Montana's unemployment rate continues to decline and our economy continues to grow," Governor Steve Bullock said. "The historic job growth we've seen since January should give Montana businesses and retailers confidence as we head to the end of the year."
"Montana's economy has posted strong gains this year for Montana's working families," said Labor Commissioner Pam Bucy. "The Department of Labor and Industry looks forward to continuing our efforts to improve the skills of the Montana workforce, ensuring that our economy will continue to out-perform in future years."
Total employment estimates suggested job growth of 580 jobs over the last month, with 12,145 jobs added over the past year. Total employment estimates include payroll employment plus agricultural and self-employed workers. The labor force increased by 56 workers in October, while the number of unemployed declined by 525 workers. Payroll employment estimates indicate a small employment decline of 400 jobs. Payroll employment has added roughly 7,000 jobs over the past year.
The Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U) increased by 0.1% in September. Increases in food and shelter prices outweighed declines in the energy index. The index for all items less food and energy increased 0.1 percent in September
