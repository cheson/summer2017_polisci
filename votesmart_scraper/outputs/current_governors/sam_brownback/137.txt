Governor Sam Brownback Makes Judicial Appointment to the 10th District Court
Press Release
By: Sam Brownback
Date: Jan. 26, 2015
Location: Unknown

Kansas Governor Sam Brownback today announced the appointment of Robert J. Wonnell of Olathe as a District Judge in the 10th District Court of the State of Kansas. Wonnell will fill a vacancy created by the retirement of Judge James Franklin Davis.
"I am pleased to appoint Robert Wonnell to the 10th District," said Governor Brownback. "I appreciate his willingness to serve the people of Kansas."
Wonnell currently practices law as a shareholder in the Kansas City firm of McAnany, Van Cleave and Phillips. He is a graduate of Phillips University in Enid, Okla., and received his law degree from the University of Kansas.
The state's 105 counties are organized into 31 Judicial Districts. The district courts serve as the trial courts for the state, overseeing all civil and criminal cases. The 10th District Court includes Johnson County.
