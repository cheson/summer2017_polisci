Gov. Rick Snyder: Mike Duggan's Vision, Leadership Valuable in Detroit's Comeback
Statement
By: Rick Snyder
Date: Dec. 19, 2013
Location: Lansing, MI

Michigan Gov. Rick Snyder offered the following statement on the announcement from Detroit Emergency Manager Kevyn Orr of an expanded role for Mayor-elect Mike Duggan.
"Bringing back Detroit to its rich glory is an enormous job that will have an impact on all of Michigan. I appreciate that Emergency Manager Kevyn Orr is reaching out to Mayor-elect Mike Duggan to tap his expertise and expand the role of the mayor. As the city's financial steward, Kevyn has tremendous responsibilities as he works in partnership to restore Detroit's fiscal health.
"The mayor-elect has extensive experience in large and complicated restructuring projects. I'm confident that he and the emergency manager will be formidable partners in tackling Detroit's challenges.
"Mike Duggan's vision and leadership will be important as he heads the city's administrative efforts, forges an economic development plan and leads efforts to remove blight.
"Michigan will truly thrive when Detroit is completely restored, from the already vibrant downtown to its neighborhoods. We're making progress and will continue to head in the right direction in partnership with Detroit's leaders."
