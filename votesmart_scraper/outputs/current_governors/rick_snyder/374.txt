Gov. Rick Snyder: Mike Flanagan Helped Students, Teachers in His Decade as State Superintendent
Statement
By: Rick Snyder
Date: June 30, 2015
Location: Lansing, MI

Gov. Rick Snyder issued the following statement upon the retirement of state Superintendent Mike Flanagan, effective today.
"Mike Flanagan devoted his career to improving the lives of young Michiganders, working to give them the best possible education -- the foundation for careers, college and a lifetime of learning. Mike put children first. As state superintendent, Mike worked to be a solid partner as we collaborated to expand early childhood programs, helping Michigan's youngest learners and their families.
"Mike advocated for higher standards for all students and expanding technology in schools. He always remembered the vital role of teachers, recognizing the hard work that goes on in Michigan classrooms everyday as educators nurture and prepare kids for a successful future.
"I wish Mike and his family well, and I'm looking forward to working with the new superintendent, Brian Whiston, as we move forward to continue improving education for all students in Michigan."
